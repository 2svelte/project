Earthquakes and Tsunamis

An earthquake occurs when a sudden release of energy in the Earth's lithosphere creates seismic waves. The 1960 Chilean earthquake is the largest earthquake that has ever been recorded (9.5 magnitudes), but the 2004 Indian Ocean earthquake is the deadliest earthquake in history. The sheer destruction caused by the 2004 Indian Ocean earthquake and tsunami killed around 230,000 people.

Data collected from the past century (as seen in the maps) shows that the Sumatran region in particular is has seen a lot of tsunamis and earthquakes of varying magnitudes, as they regularly occur off the coast of the island.

The map shows that other regions such as Japan, New Zealand, and Southern America are also accustomed to these natural disasters.

Internally displaced people are people who have been forced to leave their original countries in order to flee a damaging event such as natural disasters or conflicts.
